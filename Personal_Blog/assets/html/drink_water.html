Drinking enough water daily - the first healthy habit to follow




Starting a new healthy lifestyle may be difficult to achieve quickly and perfectly, but starting a new simple and useful habit is easy, comfortable and even exciting! If you want to start a healthy life and you did not find your way, I advise you to start from this habit They usually drink water A simple, easy, and very important habit because all the organs of our bodies need water to stay hydrated and function properly. Water is life for our bodies and plays an essential role in digestion and excretion of waste and toxins from the body. The kidneys also need it to carry out its function of purifying the blood, as well as need it. The lungs in the process of breathing.


When the body lacks water, we feel fatigue, exhaustion, headaches, as well as lethargy, and the body begins to retain the remaining water, which leads to bloating and fluid retention in the body, especially since it is difficult for the body to excrete its waste, which causes toxins to accumulate in the body. Drinking water is a very important and very simple issue, so we have to drink water frequently and in correct quantities throughout the day


How much water do I need to drink a day ?!

According to Healthline website,

Experts recommend that males consume 15.5 cups (3.7 liters) of water daily and females 11.5 cups (2.7 liters). But environmental factors such as temperature and other health conditions may affect your water needs.


Start the habit of drinking water daily

Here are some tips for upping your water game:

Flavor it:
Add fruit to your water. Lemons, limes and oranges are tried and true. Cucumber, watermelon, strawberries and herbs also are delicious options.

Tie it into a routine:
Drink a glass of water every time you brush your teeth, eat a meal or use the bathroom.

Eat it:
Many fruits and vegetables have a high water content, including melon, cucumbers, lettuce and celery.

Track it:
Invest in a high-tech bottle that connects to your smartphone and records how much you drink. Or set an alarm as a reminder and track your intake in your calendar.

Challenge a friend:
Kick off a healthy competition with a friend or your kids to see who can meet their guzzling goal most often.

Take it to go:
It can be challenging to drink enough water when you are on the go. Fill your water bottle before you leave home, and bring it along on your daily travels.

Alternate your drinks:
If you can't give up soda or juice completely, try alternating with water. Each time you finish a glass of water, switch to soda or juice and vice versa.
